## 1. Setting

### 1.1 The statement and the residue lens

Let X be a smooth projective variety over ℂ. The rational Hodge classes in
degree 2k are Hdg^k(X) = H^{2k}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{k,k}(X); the Hodge conjecture
(HC) asserts every such class is a ℚ-combination of classes of algebraic
subvarieties. For a class α, the Compiled Edition's residue lens records
the open predicate: ℐ(α) = 0 ⟺ α is algebraic. By standard spreading-out,
HC is equivalent to its restriction to varieties over ℚ̄ — countable,
effectively presentable data (Compiled Edition §25.1).

### 1.2 The frames

**Definition 1.2 (Hodge frames).** A *frame* is a finite station of any of
the following circumscription continua: enumeration of candidate algebraic
cycles over ℚ̄ of bounded degree, height, and presentation complexity, with
class comparison to bounded precision (the *search continuum*); verification
that a target class passes a finite battery of necessary conditions —
rationality, (k,k)-type, harmonicity, absolute-Hodgeness, motivated-ness
(the *filter continuum*); and closure of HC on specified loci — divisor
classes, particular families, particular strata (the *locus continuum*).
A *window observable* is any functional of such a station. The current
record stations are Tier-I facts of the Compiled Edition: all filters
passed by every surviving target (Main Theorem, component B); the fourfold
Weil stratum, the generalized-Kummer Kuga–Satake locus, and countably many
K3 families closed affirmatively (§22); the open frontier located at the
Weil sixfolds of discriminant ≠ −1, sharpest member the McMullen rigid
family (§23).

**Definition 1.3 (Frame-internal decidability; weld).** As in T1–T3
verbatim.

## 2. Classification: the inverted asymmetry

**Lemma 2.1 (Logical form; Tier I).** For a fixed target (X, α) over ℚ̄:
the positive verdict ℐ(α) = 0 is Σ-shaped over an effectively enumerable
object space — witnessed, if true, by a single finite object (a cycle with
a verification certificate) arising at some station of the search
continuum. The negative verdict ℐ(α) ≠ 0 is Π-shaped: non-membership over
the entire infinite cycle enumeration, witnessed by no station and
certifiable only by a separating invariant. The full conjecture is a
Π-statement over all instances of these Σ-statements.

*Proof.* Compiled Edition, Main Theorem component D (witness asymmetry),
restated in the tetralogy's vocabulary; the enumeration and the finite
presentability of witnesses are the spreading-out facts of §1.1. ∎

**Theorem 2.2 (One-sided frame, inverted; Tier I/II).** The Hodge search
continuum can confirm instances but can never refute one: every
frame-internal activity is, logically, witness search. This is the exact
mirror of the Riemann case (T2, Theorem 2.2), where the frame could refute
and never confirm. The two problems are the two chiralities of the same
structure: a one-sided circumscription continuum facing a tail on its
blind side. Consequently the Hodge record — every filter passed, loci
closing affirmatively decade after decade, no refutation ever produced —
is exactly the output the logical form predicts, and carries no verdict
information for the open locus beyond the witnesses it has so far failed
to find and the refutations it is structurally incapable of producing.

*Proof.* The first sentence is Lemma 2.1. The incapacity clause: a
refutation requires certifying Π-non-membership, which by Lemma 2.1 needs
an invariant; the filter continuum's members are all *necessary* conditions
of algebraicity, satisfied by the targets (Main Theorem B), hence
non-separating by construction — this is Theorem 17.6 of the Compiled
Edition, cited below as the certification prong of B⁗. ∎

**Remark 2.3 (The window is treacherous here by executed refutation;
Tier I).** As in T2 (Littlewood) and T3 (triviality; abelian
deconfinement), the domain exhibits its own proof that window extrapolation
fails — and here the exhibits are *executed refutations at the conjecture's
boundary* (Compiled Edition §36, the nil cases):

1. **Integral coefficients:** the integral Hodge conjecture is false —
   Atiyah–Hirzebruch (torsion/Steenrod filters) and Kollár (degree
   obstructions). At the integral boundary the separating invariant
   *existed*, and fired. Boundary theorem: the obstructions are torsion
   and divisibility phenomena, vanishing identically over ℚ — the filters
   that killed the neighbor provably cannot cross into the rational
   conjecture.
2. **The original general formulation:** false; refuted and corrected by
   Grothendieck.
3. **The Kähler extension:** false; Voisin's compact Kähler
   counterexamples. Boundary theorem: projectivity is load-bearing.

The surviving conjecture is the theorem-lined residue of three successful
attacks — the strongest possible form of the warning that unanimity of
window evidence (here: unanimity of filter passage) does not extrapolate,
since immediately across each boundary the same style of evidence
coexisted with refutation.
