## **EMBEDDING**

**Chord-pair hierarchy — algebraic and genuine loci distinguished throughout:**

**Level 0 — Chords of γ:** A chord of γ is a pair (p, q) with p, q ∈ γ(S¹), p ≠ q. The space of ordered distinct chord-endpoint pairs is F₂(γ) \= {(p,q) ∈ γ(S¹)² : p ≠ q}. This space is an open subset of γ(S¹)² and is NOT compact — it excludes the diagonal {p \= q}, which is a closed subset of γ(S¹)². Any compactness argument using chord pairs must specify a compactification of F₂(γ) (e.g., the closed chord space adding degenerate chords at the diagonal, or the unordered chord space) — see D9.

**Level 1 — Same-midpoint chord pairs (parallelogram candidates):** A Level-1 configuration is a pair of ordered chords (p,q), (r,s) with p,q,r,s ∈ γ(S¹), sharing a common midpoint: (p+q)/2 \= (r+s)/2.

ALGEBRAIC LOCUS L₁(γ) ⊂ γ(S¹)⁴: all quadruples (p,q,r,s) satisfying the midpoint equation, including configurations where p=q, r=s, or all four points coincide.

GENUINE LOCUS G₁(γ) ⊂ F₄(γ): algebraic locus restricted to four distinct points with p ≠ q and r ≠ s (positive-length chords). G₁(γ) consists of genuine parallelogram candidates.

Note: Level 1 follows a posteriori from Level 2\. Any four distinct points forming a rectangle already have equal-midpoint diagonals, so the Level-1 condition is subsumed by Level 2\. It is listed here for hierarchy completeness.

**Level 2 — Equal-length same-midpoint chord pairs (rectangle candidates):** A Level-2 configuration is a pair of chords sharing midpoint AND having equal length.

ALGEBRAIC LOCUS L₂(γ) ⊂ γ(S¹)⁴: all quadruples satisfying (midpoint equality AND length equality), including degenerate configurations. Defined in the closed ambient space γ(S¹)⁴, NOT in F₄(γ).

GENUINE LOCUS G\_rect(γ) ⊂ F₄(γ): algebraic locus restricted to four distinct points with positive-length chords and equal midpoint and equal length. These are genuine rectangle candidates.

STATUS OF G\_rect(γ) NONEMPTINESS: REPORTED IN LITERATURE — TIER A (Vaughan/Meyerson). Every continuous Jordan curve satisfies G\_rect(γ) ≠ ∅. T\_strong status of this result (that the rectangle configuration can be taken in cyclic order) REQUIRES VERIFICATION from the primary source.

COMPACTIFICATION: L₂(γ) is a closed subset of the compact space γ(S¹)⁴, hence compact. G\_rect(γ) ⊂ F₄(γ) is NOT automatically compact: it excludes the diagonal (coincident-point) faces of γ(S¹)⁴, which form the degeneration boundary. Limits of sequences in G\_rect(γ) may converge to configurations in L₂(γ) \\ G\_rect(γ) — configurations with coincident points satisfying the algebraic conditions vacuously.

**Level 3 — Equal-length perpendicular same-midpoint chord pairs (square candidates):** A Level-3 configuration is a Level-2 configuration where additionally the two chord directions are perpendicular.

ALGEBRAIC LOCUS L₃(γ) ⊂ γ(S¹)⁴: all quadruples satisfying midpoint equality AND length equality AND the dot product of chord directions vanishes. On the algebraic locus: perpendicularity encoded by unnormalized dot product (p−r)·(q−s) \= 0 (using unnormalized form valid at degenerate configurations). Defined in γ(S¹)⁴.

GENUINE LOCUS G\_sq(γ) ⊂ F₄(γ): algebraic locus restricted to four distinct points with positive-length diagonals. On the genuine locus, perpendicularity may equivalently be encoded by the unit-vector dot product of chord directions, since the diagonals have positive length.

STATUS OF G\_sq(γ) NONEMPTINESS: OPEN. This is the primary question of the problem.

T\_weak target: G\_sq(γ) ≠ ∅ for all continuous Jordan curves γ. T\_strong target: G\_sq(γ) ∩ C₄⁺(γ) ≠ ∅ for all continuous Jordan curves γ, where C₄⁺(γ) denotes the cyclically ordered configuration component. (See configuration space discussion below.)

*In plain terms:* Rectangles are chords that share a midpoint and have equal length — every continuous Jordan curve has at least one. Squares are rectangles whose diagonals are also perpendicular. The question is whether the "perpendicularity" step can always be achieved.

## **FLOW**

**Configuration spaces and their component structure:**

Let γ: S¹ → ℝ² be a fixed continuous Jordan curve. Since γ is a homeomorphism onto its image, γ(S¹) is homeomorphic to S¹.

**F₄(γ) \= {(v₁, v₂, v₃, v₄) ∈ γ(S¹)⁴ : vᵢ ≠ vⱼ for i ≠ j}.**

Since γ(S¹) ≅ S¹, F₄(γ) ≅ F₄(S¹) as topological spaces. F₄(S¹) has exactly 6 connected components: (4−1)\! \= 6 components, corresponding to the 6 distinct cyclic orderings of 4 labeled points on S¹. Each component corresponds to a specific cyclic order type. F₄(S¹) is NOT path-connected. (The diagonals {tᵢ \= tⱼ} in (S¹)⁴ are codimension-1 subtori of (S¹)⁴ and their complement is disconnected.)

The 6 components split as: **3 positively oriented** (cyclic orderings consistent with the positive orientation of S¹) and **3 negatively oriented** (cyclic orderings consistent with the negative orientation). Every component has a definite orientation. The 3+3=6 split is complete — no orientation-indeterminate components exist.

Positive orientation component relevant to T\_strong: **C₄⁺(γ)** — the connected component of F₄(γ) in which the four points appear in positively oriented cyclic order on γ(S¹).

PATH-CONNECTEDNESS OF C₄⁺(γ): C₄⁺(γ) ≅ S¹ × int(Δ³), where S¹ records the starting point of the cyclic configuration and int(Δ³) records the three positive gap lengths summing to 1 (basepoint-free cyclic configuration space model). This is the basepoint-free cyclic configuration space model. It is NOT the fixed-cut chamber model {(t₁,t₂,t₃,t₄): 0 ≤ t₁ \< t₂ \< t₃ \< t₄ \< 1} (which is contractible and not preserved by cyclic relabeling). C₄⁺(γ) has an S¹ factor and is not contractible.

**The square map:**

On the genuine locus, a square is characterized by its two diagonals being chords of equal length with the same midpoint, perpendicular to each other. Parametrize by the diagonal pair: for (v₁,v₂,v₃,v₄) ∈ F₄(γ), let d₁ \= v₃ − v₁ (first diagonal vector) and d₂ \= v₄ − v₂ (second diagonal vector).

The square conditions are:

**(i) Midpoint equality:** (v₁+v₃)/2 \= (v₂+v₄)/2 — equivalently, v₁+v₃ \= v₂+v₄. This contributes **2 scalar equations** in ℝ² (one per coordinate of ℝ²).

**(ii) Diagonal length equality:** |d₁|² \= |d₂|² — equivalently, |v₃−v₁|² \= |v₄−v₂|². This contributes **1 scalar equation**.

**(iii) Perpendicularity:** d₁·d₂ \= 0 — equivalently, (v₃−v₁)·(v₄−v₂) \= 0\. This is a degree-2 polynomial condition in the vertex coordinates, NOT a transcendental condition. This contributes **1 scalar equation** on the genuine locus. (On the algebraic/compactified locus, use the unnormalized dot product as written — no division by lengths required.)

Total: **4 scalar equations** on a space whose classical dimension (for the configuration of 4 ordered points on a 1-dimensional curve S¹) is 4 real parameters.

Classical codomain: **ℝ⁴** (2 components for midpoint equality in ℝ², 1 for length equality, 1 for perpendicularity).

Expected solution-set dimension under transversality: 4 − 4 \= 0\. A 0-dimensional expected solution set. DIMENSION COUNT CAVEAT: a zero-dimensional expected solution set may be empty. Dimension counting does not prove existence. This is a heuristic only.

**The complete square map (classical formulation):** Φ\_sq: F₄(γ) → ℝ⁴ with components:

* Φ₁: F₄(γ) → ℝ² : (v₁,v₂,v₃,v₄) ↦ (v₁+v₃) − (v₂+v₄) \[midpoint equality\]  
* Φ₂: F₄(γ) → ℝ : (v₁,v₂,v₃,v₄) ↦ |v₃−v₁|² − |v₄−v₂|² \[length equality\]  
* Φ₃: F₄(γ) → ℝ : (v₁,v₂,v₃,v₄) ↦ (v₃−v₁)·(v₄−v₂) \[perpendicularity\]

Type check: Φ₁ is ℝ²-valued (2 scalar dimensions); Φ₂ is ℝ-valued (1 scalar dimension); Φ₃ is ℝ-valued (1 scalar dimension). Total codomain dimension: 2+1+1 \= 4\. ✓

All four components are continuous on F₄(γ). Φ₃ is a degree-2 polynomial in the vᵢ coordinates. Φ₃ is defined on the entire ambient space γ(S¹)⁴ (no division by lengths), so it extends continuously to the algebraic locus including degenerate configurations.

The zero set Φ\_sq⁻¹(0) ∩ F₄(γ) is the algebraic square locus intersected with the distinct-point space. This zero set equals G\_sq(γ) ∪ (possible non-square configurations with all conditions algebraically satisfied but with some degeneracy) — the zero-set correspondence must be verified on the genuine locus.

Zero-set correspondence on genuine locus (four distinct points, positive-length diagonals): a point (v₁,v₂,v₃,v₄) ∈ F₄(γ) is in Φ\_sq⁻¹(0) if and only if the two diagonals have the same midpoint, the same length, and are perpendicular. Four distinct points with equal bisecting diagonals of equal length that are perpendicular form a genuine square. ZERO-SET CORRESPONDENCE ON GENUINE LOCUS: EXACT (for Φ\_sq on F₄(γ)). ✓

For T\_strong, the relevant domain is C₄⁺(γ) ⊂ F₄(γ) — the cyclically ordered component. Φ\_sq restricted to C₄⁺(γ) provides the T\_strong test.

*In plain terms:* The square conditions — equal and bisecting diagonals that are also perpendicular — translate into four scalar equations on the coordinates of the four candidate points. The map that encodes these four conditions is continuous and well-defined. The open question is whether it always has a zero.
