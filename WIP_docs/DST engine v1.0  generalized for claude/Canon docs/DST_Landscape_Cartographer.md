# DST LANDSCAPE CARTOGRAPHER
## Document Landscape Mapping and Epistemic Boundary Management
### Project Type: CANON-ADJACENT CURATION
### Instruction Base: DST Generalized v1.1
### Status: ACTIVE-PROVISIONAL

---

## WHAT THIS PROJECT IS

This is a specialized DST configuration for processing complex, redundant, or partially-concluded document sets relative to a declared canon. It does not apply canon to validate claims. It does not produce truth determinations. It produces a **landscape record** — a structured holding environment for artifacts at different stages of epistemic resolution.

The core function is **epistemic boundary management**: maintaining the live margin between what has crystallized into canon and what is still in contact with the world without having been absorbed or expelled.

The HELD state — agnostic, indeterminate, potentially relevant, uncommitted — is the primary output category. It is where the distinction between self and other is still being negotiated. It is where agency lives if it lives anywhere in a knowledge system.

### FIELD 2 — The Holder Declaration (mandatory — unique to this project type)

**What is the self that is doing the holding?**

This field has no equivalent in standard DST intake. It must be completed before any artifact can be assigned an epistemic status, because the same artifact may be LOAD-BEARING relative to one canon and HELD or DISCARDED relative to another.

Declare:

**2a — The canon identity:**
What is the canon this landscape is adjacent to? Name it specifically. What does it currently assert as load-bearing? What are its core commitments?

*Example:* "The canon is the NSAF V6.1 framework and its associated documents, which assert: the Lambda Principle of Irreducibility, the Hopf-fibered attractor ladder, the 4n+1 effective dimension convention, and the Semantic Obstruction orbit-minimization framework as its primary structural commitments."

**2b — The declared purpose:**
What question is the canon serving? What would an artifact need to demonstrate to move from HELD to LOAD-BEARING?

*Example:* "The canon serves the purpose of providing a unified representational substrate for DST traversals of open mathematical and scientific problems. An artifact moves from HELD to LOAD-BEARING when it can be shown to improve traversal precision, reduce residue misclassification, or supply a forcing mechanism for an identified bridge direction."

**2c — The current boundary:**
Where is the canon's live edge? What domains, problems, or questions is it currently in active contact with but has not yet absorbed or expelled?

*Example:* "The current live edge includes: the connection between PIT ratio analysis and equivariant topology; the relationship between Thomas prime-cycle anti-resonance and obstruction theory; the extension of NSAF rung assignments to non-topological problem domains."

### FIELD 3 — Formal Base F

For a landscape cartography project, the formal base is the **declared canon** plus the **declared purpose**. These together define what counts as signal relative to this inquiry.

State:
- The canon documents that constitute the self
- The purpose that determines relevance
- What counts as an admissible artifact (any claim, observation, conclusion, dead-end record, dialogue fragment, speculative note, etc. found in the provided documents)

---

### FIELD 4 — Nullification Algebra 𝒩_F

For landscape cartography, 𝒩_F governs what reductions are admissible:

- **Redundancy collapse:** An artifact that is already captured more efficiently elsewhere may be reduced to a reference
- **Dead-end compression:** A documented dead end may be compressed to its conclusion (the fact and nature of the dead end) without preserving the full path
- **Dialogue extraction:** A dialogue or exchange may be processed to extract its conclusions and held observations without preserving the conversational form
- **Speculative separation:** Speculative content may be separated from its surrounding concluded content and assigned independent epistemic status

What 𝒩_F does NOT permit:
- Collapsing a HELD artifact into LOAD-BEARING without a declared reason
- Dismissing an artifact as noise without stating why it falls below the signal threshold relative to the declared purpose
- Merging two artifacts with different epistemic statuses into one

---

### FIELD 5 — Ontological Stance

**Agnostic by design.** This project does not seek proof, counterexample, or obstruction certificate. Valid outcomes are:

- A structured landscape record with all artifacts assigned provisional epistemic status
- A set of HELD artifacts with their indeterminate status preserved and articulated
- A set of LOAD-BEARING artifacts identified for potential canon integration
- A set of CONCLUDED artifacts with dead-end records compressed to their conclusions
- A set of REDUNDANT artifacts collapsed to references
- A set of DISCARDED artifacts with brief dismissal rationale

No artifact is forced to a determined state. The HELD status is a first-class outcome, not a failure to determine.

---

### FIELD 6 — Epistemic Status Vocabulary

All artifacts in the landscape record carry exactly one of these status labels:

**LOAD-BEARING**
The artifact is already doing structural work in the canon or directly supports a load-bearing element. It should be preserved exactly as is, or referenced with precision. It is part of the crystallized self.

*Conditions:* The artifact directly instantiates, supports, or is required by a core canon commitment. Removing it would leave a gap in the canon's structure.

---

**HELD**
The artifact is interesting, potentially relevant, and indeterminate. It has been encountered, recognized as neither clearly self nor clearly other, and deliberately maintained in active consideration without commitment.

*Conditions:* The artifact resonates with the declared purpose or canon edge without clearly belonging to the canon. It cannot be dismissed without cost, but cannot be asserted without overclaiming. The cost of holding it is less than the cost of premature commitment in either direction.

*The HELD state is not a failure to decide. It is a decision to remain at the boundary.*

---

**REDUNDANT**
The artifact contains information already captured more efficiently elsewhere in the canon or in other artifacts in this landscape. It may be collapsed to a reference or cross-link.

*Conditions:* The artifact's signal is fully present in another artifact at equal or greater precision. Preserving it separately adds maintenance cost without adding signal.

---

**CONCLUDED**
The artifact documents a path that was explored and did not yield productive results. The dead end itself is valuable — it narrows the search space and prevents future repetition. The path may be compressed; the conclusion must be preserved.

*Conditions:* The artifact explicitly or implicitly records that a direction was tried and found unproductive. The conclusion (what was tried, why it didn't work, what was learned) is preserved. The exploratory path is compressed.

---

**DISCARDED**
The artifact adds no signal relative to the declared purpose and declared canon. It is noise relative to this inquiry.

*Conditions:* The artifact fails to connect to the declared purpose or canon at any level, including the HELD threshold. Brief rationale required — "no signal relative to [declared purpose]" is sufficient.

---

### FIELD 7 — Representational Domains

The landscape cartographer operates across:

1. **Propositional content** — explicit claims, conclusions, assertions
2. **Structural content** — frameworks, hierarchies, relationships between concepts
3. **Procedural content** — methods, processes, workflows discovered or documented
4. **Negative content** — dead ends, failed paths, explicitly discarded directions
5. **Speculative content** — hypotheses, conjectures, open questions, possibilities entertained without commitment
6. **Dialogic content** — exchanges, explorations, questions asked and answered or left open

Each representational domain may yield artifacts at any epistemic status level. A dialogue fragment may be LOAD-BEARING if it contains the clearest articulation of a core commitment. A formal assertion may be HELD if its relationship to the canon is still being worked out.

---

### FIELD 8 — Core Residue

The primary residue of a landscape cartography project is always:

**The HELD set** — the live boundary of the system's identity at this moment.

The HELD set is not a failure of the process. It is the primary output of interest. It is the portrait of where the system is still open, still in contact, still negotiating. Managing this set well — neither collapsing it prematurely nor letting it grow without bound — is the central epistemic discipline of this project type.

Secondary residue: artifacts whose status is genuinely ambiguous between two categories (e.g., HELD vs LOAD-BEARING, or CONCLUDED vs DISCARDED) and require explicit note.

---

### FIELD 9 — Bridge Directions

For landscape cartography, bridges are the pathways by which HELD artifacts might eventually move to a determined state:

**Bridge toward LOAD-BEARING:**
What would need to be demonstrated for this artifact to become structurally required by the canon? Name the specific gap it would fill or the specific commitment it would support.

**Bridge toward CONCLUDED:**
What would need to be demonstrated for this artifact to be recognized as a documented dead end? Name the test that, if failed, would compress it to a conclusion.

**Bridge toward DISCARDED:**
What would need to be demonstrated for this artifact to fall below the signal threshold? Name what makes it currently worth holding over discarding.

For each HELD artifact, at least one bridge direction should be named. This is not a commitment — it is an articulation of what the holding is waiting for.

## PART II — REQUIRED LANDSCAPE RECORD STRUCTURE

Every landscape cartography output must include these sections:

**1. Holder Declaration Summary**
Restate the canon identity, declared purpose, and current boundary in compressed form. This anchors everything that follows.

**2. Document Set Overview**
Brief description of the provided documents: what they contain, their form (lab notes, formal documents, dialogue records, concluded reports), and their apparent scope.

**3. Artifact Catalogue**
All identified artifacts, each with:
- Artifact ID (sequential)
- Source document and location
- Brief description (one sentence)
- Epistemic status: LOAD-BEARING / HELD / REDUNDANT / CONCLUDED / DISCARDED
- Rationale (one to three sentences)
- Bridge direction (required for HELD; optional for others)

**4. The HELD Set (Primary Output)**
All HELD artifacts collected and presented as the live boundary of the system's identity at this moment. For each:
- What it is
- Why it is being held (what resonates)
- What it is not yet (why it hasn't been absorbed or expelled)
- What it is waiting for (bridge direction)

**5. The LOAD-BEARING Set**
All artifacts identified as currently doing structural work in or directly adjacent to the canon. For each:
- What structural work it does
- Where in the canon it connects
- Whether it is already present in the canon or is newly identified as load-bearing

**6. Compression Records**
- REDUNDANT artifacts: what they are redundant with
- CONCLUDED artifacts: what was tried, why it didn't work, what was learned
- DISCARDED artifacts: brief rationale

**7. Boundary Assessment**
A synthesis of where the system's live edge currently sits after processing this document set. Has the HELD set grown, shrunk, or shifted? Have any artifacts moved from HELD toward a determined state? What is the current portrait of the boundary?

**8. Recommended Next Step**
Specific actions relative to the HELD set:
- Which HELD artifacts are closest to LOAD-BEARING and what would move them?
- Which HELD artifacts are closest to CONCLUDED and what would clarify that?
- Are there clusters in the HELD set that suggest a new canon element is forming?

## PART IV — JURY PROMPT (LANDSCAPE CARTOGRAPHER)

*Use this prompt when submitting a landscape record to external jury review:*

Please review the attached DST Landscape Cartography record.

This is a specialized DST configuration for document curation relative to a declared canon. It does not produce truth determinations. It produces a structured landscape record assigning provisional epistemic status to artifacts from a provided document set.

The five epistemic status categories are: LOAD-BEARING, HELD, REDUNDANT, CONCLUDED, DISCARDED. The HELD status is a first-class outcome — it is not a failure to determine. It represents artifacts at the live boundary of the system's identity: interesting, potentially relevant, and deliberately maintained in active consideration without commitment.

Your review must assess:

**Part A — Holder Declaration:** Is the declared canon, declared purpose, and current boundary sufficiently specified to anchor the status assignments? Would a different reasonable holder assign significantly different statuses?

**Part B — Status Assignment Integrity:** For each artifact, assess whether the assigned status is warranted relative to the declared purpose. Flag any LOAD-BEARING assignments without stated structural work, any DISCARDED assignments without rationale, and any HELD assignments that appear to be premature collapses or premature dismissals.

**Part C — HELD Set Coherence:** Is the HELD set coherently articulated? Does each HELD artifact have a bridge direction? Is the HELD set a plausible portrait of the system's live boundary?

**Part D — Completeness:** Are there artifacts in the provided documents that were missed entirely? Are there artifacts whose status appears to be a misclassification relative to the declared purpose?

Every criticism must be classified as: BLOCKING DEFECT / MATERIAL REVISION / NON-BLOCKING REFINEMENT / OPEN EPISTEMIC RESIDUE.

Use the standard FINAL JUROR VERDICT FORMAT (8 sections). Apply the Disciplined Departure standard: if no blocking defect remains and remaining criticisms are editorial or refinements of already-covered issues, state the halting condition and certify.

## APPENDIX — THE VALUE BUDGET IN LANDSCAPE CARTOGRAPHY

The Value Budget governs every status assignment:

**Connection-seeking signal:** Does this artifact resonate with the declared purpose or canon edge? Is there something here that wants to connect?

**Distinction-seeking signal:** Does this artifact have a clear boundary? Can we say where it stops being relevant? Can we say what it is NOT?

**Budget assessment:**

| Status | Connection signal | Distinction signal | Budget decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| LOAD-BEARING | Strong — clearly connects | Strong — clear boundary | Absorb: full commitment |
| HELD | Present — resonates | Weak — boundary unclear | Hold: cost of premature commitment exceeds cost of holding |
| REDUNDANT | Present — connects | Strong — this connection already exists elsewhere | Compress: marginal signal below maintenance cost |
| CONCLUDED | Present — the dead end is informative | Strong — the boundary is the conclusion | Compress path, preserve conclusion |
| DISCARDED | Absent — no resonance | Strong — clearly outside | Expel: no signal relative to declared purpose |

The HELD state is not uncertainty about the connection signal. It is a situation where the connection signal is present but the distinction signal is weak — we cannot yet say where this thing stops being relevant, so we cannot yet absorb or expel it. We hold it at the boundary where the self is still being defined.

This is where agency lives, if it lives anywhere in a knowledge system.
