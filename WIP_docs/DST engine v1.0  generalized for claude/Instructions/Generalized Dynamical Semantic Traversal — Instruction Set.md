\#\# HOW TO USE THESE INSTRUCTIONS

These instructions govern any DST traversal. They contain no problem-specific content. All references to specific domains, conditions, or configurations are replaced with abstract slots marked \[LIKE THIS\].

At the start of every new project, complete the INTAKE TEMPLATE (Part I) to declare the problem-specific content. The meta-rules (Parts II–XI) then apply to that content throughout the traversal.

The canon frameworks (DST v1.1b, NSAF V6.1, Lambda Principle, Semantic Obstruction, Pythagorean Interval Theory, TUFT) remain active as the primary structural substrate unless the project explicitly declares a different or supplementary canon.

\# PART II — META-RULE A: SCOPE CLAIMS CORRECTLY

\*\*A1 — Scope claims to what was actually argued.\*\*

Do not assert that a bridge closes a condition it does not directly address. Scope every bridge closure to exactly what was argued.

\*\*A2 — Scope mechanism claims to verified mechanisms.\*\*

Do not credit a named argument or theorem with a mechanism it has not been verified to use. If the mechanism is uncertain, flag it for verification and rely only on the stated output.

\*\*A3 — Scope absence claims to the traversal, not to the field.\*\*

"This traversal has not supplied X" is correct. "X does not exist" requires a proof or cited theorem.

\*\*A4 — Scope absence claims to the traversal scope.\*\*

All absence claims use the form "this traversal has not supplied X" or "not established within this traversal."

\*\*A5 — Scope temporal claims to verified knowledge.\*\*

Do not assert currency of literature claims without live verification. Flag all current-literature claims for verification before public release.

\*\*A6 — Scope exhaustion claims to declared purpose.\*\*

A bridge may be labeled EXHAUSTED FOR \[SPECIFIC PURPOSE\] only. It is not exhausted absolutely. The exact purpose for which it is exhausted must be named.

\*\*A7 — No universal invisibility claims.\*\*

Do not assert that a whole class of methods cannot detect, see, control, or prove a condition unless a theorem proving that impossibility is supplied. Scope all limitations to the specific bridges surveyed.

\*\*A8 — Logical formalization scope rule.\*\*

When a traversal describes a problem as "first-order expressible" or uses equivalent logical formalization language, it must distinguish:

(a) The configuration-level claim: the condition on a fixed finite set of objects is algebraic and expressible in the language of ordered fields.

(b) The universal problem-level claim: the statement "for every \[object\], there exist \[configuration\] satisfying \[condition\]" quantifies universally and requires set-theoretic topology or equivalent ambient foundation.

\*\*A9 — Obstruction exhaustion discipline.\*\*

A bridge may not be labeled EXHAUSTED for obstruction-certificate production unless the traversal has: (a) specified the obstruction theory surveyed; (b) identified the candidate class or class family; (c) specified the domain and coefficient system; (d) performed a computation or cited a theorem. If none of these are supplied, the correct label is: "NO OBSTRUCTION CERTIFICATE PRODUCED IN THIS TRAVERSAL."

\*\*A10 — Declared target versus literature target.\*\*

When a traversal declares a target that is stronger than, weaker than, or convention-dependent relative to the most common literature formulation, the traversal must explicitly distinguish the declared target from the standard literature formulation. It may not call the declared target "the standard problem" unless it cites or proves equivalence.

\# PART III — META-RULE B: DISTINGUISH LOGICAL IMPLICATION STRENGTH

\*\*B1 — Separate implication, proof transfer, and strictness.\*\*

(1) Logical implication: a target with all requirements of another plus additional requirements implies the weaker target.

(2) Proof-transfer limitation: a proof of the weaker target does not automatically prove the stronger unless additional requirements are separately established.

(3) Strictness: "the converse fails" requires a counterexample or cited theorem. If the converse has not been proved or disproved, the required label is "not established in this traversal."

\*\*B2 — A theorem proves exactly what its statement says.\*\*

Do not credit a theorem with closing a condition stronger than what appears in its statement.

\*\*B3 — Distinguish geometric or conceptual predicates from their algebraic encodings.\*\*

Always distinguish "the algebraic equation is satisfied" from "the genuine condition holds."

\*\*B4 — Characterize subsets with respect to their ambient topology.\*\*

Always specify the ambient space when stating a subset is open, closed, compact, or connected.

\*\*B5 — Every level has an explicit independent status label.\*\*

Every target, every level in a hierarchy, every bridge has its own status label. Labels do not transfer automatically between levels or targets.

\*\*B6 — Plain-language summaries inherit verification flags.\*\*

No plain-language or summary section may state a provisional result more strongly than the formal section from which it derives.

\*\*B7 — Endpoint-IVT rule.\*\*

An IVT argument must use a signed function whose target value lies strictly in the interior of the function's range, not at an endpoint. Verify that the function is signed (takes both positive and negative values) and that the target is not an endpoint of the codomain.

\*\*B8 — IVT must operate on genuine satisfaction locus, not full configuration space.\*\*

An IVT argument for a residue condition must operate on a connected family of genuine configurations that already satisfy all prior-level conditions — not on the full configuration space of arbitrary configurations.

\*\*B9 — Target-reduction-scope rule.\*\*

Any statement that "the problem reduces to \[condition X\]" must declare whether the reduction targets the primary target, the secondary target, or both. A reduction on one target does not automatically serve the other.

\*\*B10 — Signed IVT double requirement.\*\*

An IVT bridge on a restricted genuine locus requires BOTH: (a) existence of two configurations with opposite signs of the signed function; AND (b) path-connectedness of the relevant domain. Neither condition alone suffices. Both must be named as open in every summary of the IVT bridge gap.

\*\*B11 — IVT gap propagation rule.\*\*

Whenever an IVT bridge is summarized outside its detailed section — in the abstract, ontological gain, or residue certificate — the summary must name BOTH prerequisites as distinct items whenever either is unestablished.

\# PART IV — META-RULE C: ABSENCE OF EVIDENCE VS EVIDENCE OF ABSENCE

\*\*C1 — Missing construction ≠ impossibility.\*\*

The absence of a supplied bridge, proof, or construction is not evidence that no such thing exists. State: "This traversal has not supplied X." Do not state: "X does not exist."

\*\*C2 — Failed bridge ≠ closed domain.\*\*

A bridge that does not close a condition for a given purpose does not imply that no bridge of that type can ever close it. Scope the failure to the specific bridge surveyed.

\*\*C3 — Equivariant maps must be stated before quotienting.\*\*

Any equivariant map must be formulated on its domain before any quotient structure is applied. Do not present a quotient map as the equivariant object.

\# PART V — META-RULE D: ALGEBRAIC VS GENUINE SATISFACTION

\*\*D1 — Name algebraic and genuine loci distinctly.\*\*

The algebraic locus \[A\_X\] and the genuine locus \[G\_X\] must be named separately throughout. They are different objects.

\*\*D2 — State compactness properties correctly.\*\*

The algebraic locus is typically closed in a compact ambient space (hence compact). The genuine locus excludes the degeneration boundary and is typically not compact. State both properties explicitly.

\*\*D3 — Degenerate configurations satisfy algebraic conditions vacuously.\*\*

A configuration with coincident elements or zero-measure components satisfies algebraic polynomial conditions trivially. This is not genuine satisfaction.

\*\*D4 — Algebraic nonempty does not imply genuine nonempty.\*\*

The algebraic locus is always nonempty (trivial/degenerate configurations exist). The genuine locus may be empty. Never infer genuine nonemptiness from algebraic nonemptiness.

\*\*D5 — Diagnose compactification failure mode.\*\*

When genuine nonemptiness is at stake, identify the specific degeneration-boundary failure mode: what type of degeneration (collision, collapse, coincidence) would prevent genuine satisfaction in the limit?

\*\*D6 — All bridge entries include genuine satisfaction status.\*\*

Every bridge in the catalogue must include GENUINE SATISFACTION STATUS with the specific degeneration mode named.

\*\*D7 — Track parameters in all limit arguments.\*\*

Any limiting argument must track parameter representatives: write \[vₙ,ᵢ \= γₙ(tₙ,ᵢ)\], extract convergent subsequences in the compact parameter space, and use uniform convergence to conclude limit points satisfy the target conditions.

\*\*D8 — Compactness does not establish genuine nonemptiness.\*\*

Compactness may be invoked to extract convergent subsequences from existing sequences. It does not supply those sequences. The source of the sequence must be separately established.

\*\*D9 — Non-compact spaces require explicit compactification.\*\*

Any space that excludes a degenerate boundary (coincident pairs, zero-measure elements) is open in the ambient product space and NOT compact. Any compactness argument must specify the compactification.

\*\*D10 — Algebraic loci are defined in closed ambient spaces.\*\*

Define all algebraic loci in the closed ambient product space. Define genuine loci as algebraic loci intersected with the distinct-element configuration space.

\*\*D11 — Pairwise distinctness notation rule.\*\*

Chained inequality notation (v₁ ≠ v₂ ≠ v₃ ≠ v₄) is FORBIDDEN as a substitute for pairwise distinctness — it asserts only adjacent terms differ. Use: "pairwise distinct" or "vᵢ ≠ vⱼ for all i ≠ j." Exception: this notation may appear as a quoted forbidden example inside this audit rule only, with explicit statement that it appears nowhere else as an operative condition.

\*\*E — Final outputs only.\*\*

Remove scratch work, false starts, self-corrections, and mid-sentence repairs. Uncertainty belongs in status labels, not in narrative self-correction.

\*\*F1 — All established results include exact regularity class or explicit flag.\*\*

\*\*F2 — Use the most current relied-upon citation for each result.\*\*

\*\*F3 — Do not conflate regularity strata.\*\*

\*\*F4 — RETIRED.\*\* (Subsumed by F3 and F7 in v25 generalization.)

\*\*F5 — Verification flags match status labels throughout.\*\*

\*\*F6 — Current-literature verification required before public release.\*\*

\*\*F7 — Scope any result to exactly what it establishes.\*\*

Distinguish: (a) existence of at least one \[configuration\]; (b) existence of a positive-measure family; (c) existence of \[configuration\] for every prescribed \[parameter\]. Do not attribute (b) or (c) to a result that only establishes (a).

\*\*G1-G7 — All maps fully type-checked.\*\*

Every map must have: domain stated, codomain stated, all component types stated, scalar dimension count verified.

\*\*G8 — Use unnormalized forms on algebraic/degenerate loci.\*\*

On loci that include configurations with zero-measure elements, use the unnormalized form of any condition that involves division or normalization.

\*\*G9 — Dimension counting does not prove existence.\*\*

A zero-dimensional expected solution set may be empty. Every dimension count argument must include this caveat.

\*\*G10 — Propagate dimension corrections globally.\*\*

If a dimension claim is corrected at one site, the correction must propagate to all other sites before the audit can PASS.

\*\*I1 — Named arguments described by mechanisms, not slogans.\*\*

\*\*I2 — Theorem output and proof mechanism must be separated.\*\*

\*\*I4 — Do not reduce named arguments to false slogans.\*\*

\*\*J1 — Name specific degeneration modes.\*\*

Every degeneracy discussion identifies the specific mode: coincident elements, zero-measure components, limit parameter coincidence, etc.

\*\*J2 — Do not repeat excluded degenerations as risks.\*\*

Once a degeneration mode has been excluded by the construction or domain definition, do not list it again as an independent risk.

\*\*J4 — Collinearity or analogous generic position issues.\*\*

When two distinct positive-measure equal-condition elements are established, verify whether collinearity (or equivalent generic-position failure) is an independent risk. If the conditions exclude it, state this explicitly.

\# PART VII — META-RULE H: GROUP ACTIONS, QUOTIENTS, EQUIVARIANCE

\*\*H1 — State all equivariant bridge ingredients explicitly.\*\*

For any equivariant bridge: state the group, the domain, the domain action, the codomain representation, and the equivariance condition.

\*\*H2 — Define equivariant maps before quotienting.\*\*

The equivariant map must be defined on the domain before any quotient structure is applied.

\*\*H4/H10 — Classify zero-set correspondence explicitly.\*\*

For every equivariant test map, classify the zero-set correspondence as: EXACT / SUPERSET / SUBSET / UNVERIFIED.

\*\*H8 — Freeness on ordered configurations.\*\*

The group action on ordered distinct configurations is free if no nontrivial group element fixes any ordered tuple with all elements distinct. Geometric symmetries of unlabeled configurations are NOT stabilizers in the ordered configuration space.

\*\*H9 — Target-appropriate domain for each target.\*\*

If a target requires a specific structural property (cyclic order, orientation, etc.), the domain must encode that property. A domain without that encoding cannot serve that target.

\*\*H11 — Cyclic configuration space model.\*\*

For cyclically-ordered configuration spaces: use the basepoint-free model (product of a circle and an open simplex), NOT the fixed-cut chamber (which is contractible). State which model is being used.

\*\*H12 — Orientation behavior of group elements.\*\*

State explicitly which group elements preserve the chosen domain component and which map it to other components. If only a subgroup preserves the component, the bridge is a bridge for that subgroup, not the full group.

\*\*H13 — Compactification of cyclic configuration spaces.\*\*

The natural compactification of the basepoint-free cyclic model uses gap coordinates, allowing gap lengths to approach zero. Identify the degeneration faces of this compactification explicitly.

\*\*H14 — Exact-order elements, not subgroup membership.\*\*

When a residue condition requires membership in a specific set of exact-order elements within a group, state the exact-order set explicitly. Do not substitute subgroup membership language that includes elements of other orders.

\*\*H15 — Exact-order-4 elements, not full Z/4 subgroup.\*\*

The condition \[direction ratio ∈ {i, −i}\] requires the exact-order-4 elements. Elements of order 1 and order 2 (e.g., 1 and −1) correspond to parallel/antiparallel configurations and must be explicitly excluded.

\*\*H16 — Representation adequacy for torsion conditions.\*\*

When a bridge uses a group representation to encode a torsion condition, verify that the representation is faithful to the relevant torsion order. A real 1-dimensional sign representation factors through Z/2 and cannot encode order-4 data. State the representation dimension separately from the scalar equation count.

\*\*H17 — Total representation for equivariant maps.\*\*

An equivariant bridge must specify the total target representation encoding ALL conditions of the target configuration — not just the residue condition in isolation.

\*\*H18 — Group-action/domain compatibility.\*\*

Before proposing an equivariant bridge, verify that the named group acts on the stated domain. If only a subgroup preserves the chosen domain component, the bridge must be labeled by that subgroup. The acting group, domain, and well-definedness of the action must be stated before any equivariant argument proceeds.

\# PART IX — META-RULES M, N: CONTINUITY, COMPACTNESS, RESIDUE CLASSIFICATION

\*\*M1 — IVT certification.\*\*

Before stating an IVT argument, certify: (a) the function; (b) the continuity domain; (c) two domain points with opposite function values; (d) the target value lies strictly between them; (e) the domain is path-connected.

\*\*M2 — Compactness argument certification.\*\*

Before invoking compactness: identify the sequence, the compact space, and the source of the sequence separately from the compactness invocation.

\*\*M3 — Path-connectedness requires proof or citation.\*\*

Path-connectedness may be discharged inline with a brief proof. It must not be assumed without argument.

\*\*M4 — Limit-passage continuity requirement.\*\*

Any limit-passage argument must specify the continuity mechanism (uniform convergence, polynomial continuity, etc.) that allows the limit to inherit the target conditions.

\*\*M5 — Elementary topology discharged inline.\*\*

Elementary topological facts (compactness of closed subsets of compact spaces, path-connectedness of products of path-connected spaces, etc.) may be discharged inline with a one-sentence proof.

\*\*N1 — Apply PIT ratio analysis before metric classification.\*\*

Before classifying a residue as METRIC RESIDUE, check whether the condition has a commensurate ratio representation in a closed continuum. A rational proportion of a closed continuum is commensurate — it is not the same class of condition as an irrational length comparison.

\*\*N2 — Apply Lambda analysis before metric classification.\*\*

Check whether the apparent metric character of a condition is a C/L encoding artifact — the result of representing a continuous-frame condition in a linear-frame encoding.

\*\*N3 — Encoding classification required.\*\*

If the residue type changes under an alternative encoding available in the canon, the classification is ENCODING-DEPENDENT, not ENCODING-INVARIANT. Reclassify before declaring irreducibility.

\*\*N4 — Group-theoretic encoding assessment.\*\*

If a residue condition has a group-theoretic encoding, assess whether that encoding is: (a) formally equivalent to the original on the genuine locus (ESTABLISH); (b) tractable by group-theoretic methods (SEPARATELY ASSESS — formal equivalence does not imply tractability).

\*\*N7 — Formal equivalence and tractability kept strictly separate.\*\*

Never conflate the establishment of formal equivalence between two encodings with the establishment of tractability by methods natural to the new encoding. State both separately. Tractability requires a specific forcing theorem.

\*\*N8 — Algebraic conditions are not transcendental.\*\*

A polynomial condition in the coordinates of a configuration is algebraic. Do not use the word transcendental for such conditions.

\*\*N9 — Encoding equivalence does not eliminate geometric content.\*\*

Establishing that a condition has a group-theoretic encoding does not eliminate the geometric content of the condition. The geometric content is preserved; only its representational form has changed.

\*\*N10 — Positive measure does not imply target inclusion.\*\*

A positive-measure set of parameter values does not imply that any specific target parameter value belongs to the set, to its closure, or to any identified sub-interval. A separate argument is required.

\*\*N11 — Positive-measure parameter interval scope rule.\*\*

When registering a positive-measure result, state the exact interval from which the positive-measure set is drawn, as copied from the cited source.

\*\*N12 — Representation/zero-test separation.\*\*

A scalar zero-test (a polynomial condition that vanishes on the genuine locus) and a faithful group representation (required for equivariant forcing theorems) are distinct objects. A scalar zero-test may correctly detect genuine configurations without being a faithful representation of the relevant group action.

\*\*N12b — Scalar equation count and representation dimension stated separately.\*\*

The scalar equation count (number of conditions) and the faithful representation dimension (real or complex dimension of the representation space) are different quantities serving different roles. State both separately.

\# PART X — META-RULES P, Q, R, S: SYNTHESIS, COMPONENTS, INTEGRITY

\*\*P1 — Integrated canon synthesis required before isolated observations.\*\*

Before stating individual canon framework observations, produce an integrated synthesis of what all canon tools together are reading in the residue structure. The synthesis comes first.

\*\*P2 — Convergence is a signal, not a proof.\*\*

When multiple canon tools independently identify the same residue structure or bridge direction, state this convergence explicitly as the strongest heuristic signal the framework produces. Distinguish convergence from coincidence and from proof.

\*\*P3 — Integrated reading follows canonical sequence.\*\*

The integrated reading proceeds: (relevant ratio/encoding analysis) → (structural scaffold assignment) → (irreducibility/torsion check) → (orbit-minimized residue assessment) → (synthesis) → (unified disclaimer).

\*\*P4 — Heuristic scaffolding section structure.\*\*

Structure: (a) integrated synthesis first; (b) individual framework contributions as facets of a single lens; (c) unified disclaimer applied once to the synthesis as a whole — not repeated after each observation.

\*\*P5 — Canon unity declaration in Declared Fields.\*\*

The Declared Fields section must include an explicit statement that the canon frameworks are applied as a unified descriptive system, not as independent tools.

\*\*P6 — Specify the bridge that converts the canon signal to proof-level closure.\*\*

The integrated synthesis must name the specific construction that would convert the integrated heuristic signal into proof-level closure.

\*\*P7 — Traversability vocabulary restriction.\*\*

"Traversable" in heuristic scaffolding means: not identified by the relevant model as a maximal anti-resonance anchor. This must be immediately defined and immediately disclaimed as not implying a bridge exists or tractability is established.

\*\*Q1 — Configuration space component accounting.\*\*

The ordered configuration space of n labeled distinct elements on a circle is NOT path-connected for n ≥ 3\. Its components are determined by the cyclic order of the labels. State the component count and its derivation before any path-connectedness claim.

\*\*Q2 — Path-connectedness claimed only for specific components.\*\*

Path-connectedness may be claimed only for a specific named component, with an inline proof.

\*\*Q3 — All topology arguments specify the component.\*\*

Before any IVT, equivariant, or forcing argument, state: which component is being used; how the group action affects that component; and the scope of the argument within that component.

\*\*Q4 — Component count verified in audit.\*\*

For every configuration space invoked, the audit must verify: the number of connected components, and which component(s) the traversal operates on.

\*\*Q5 — Component split must sum to declared total.\*\*

When components are partitioned into sub-classes, the sub-class counts must sum exactly to the total declared component count.

\*\*Q6 — Cyclic component labeling convention.\*\*

When a specific cyclic-order component is selected for a target requiring cyclic order, that component must be identified as the single component compatible with the declared orientation and labeling convention — not as the union of all same-orientation components. State the labeling convention explicitly.

\*\*Q7 — Orientation-component convention rule.\*\*

The "positive/negative" orientation split of cyclic configuration space components is convention-dependent. It is not an intrinsic parity label. The labeling convention (orientation of the base space, vertex labeling order) must be stated before any component is called "positive" or "negative."

\*\*R1 — Geometric shorthand permitted; classificatory use of unconstructed structures is not.\*\*

Standard geometric shorthand ("circle-valued function," "fiber-like parameter") is permitted when no theorem-level consequence is inferred from an unconstructed structure. An unconstructed bundle or fibration must not be cited as formal support for a residue classification, forcing theorem, or bridge status.

\*\*R2 — Formal use of bundles requires full specification.\*\*

If a bundle or fibration is used to support a formal conclusion, specify: total space, base space, fiber, projection map, group action, and why the structure is a genuine bundle.

\*\*R3 — Bundle construction as open item.\*\*

If a bridge requires a bundle, "construct the bundle" must appear as an explicit open item in the bridge construction checklist.

\*\*S1 — Heuristic grammar rule.\*\*

All heuristic sections use conditional/modeling language. No unconstructed structure asserted as existing. Unified disclaimer applied once to the integrated synthesis.

\*\*S9 — External theorem status consistency.\*\*

No bare ESTABLISHED label in the abstract or summary may conflict with PROVISIONAL for the same claim elsewhere.

\*\*S10 — Public-release gate consistency.\*\*

If any section states "before public release, X must be verified," the audit summary may not certify public readiness. It may certify only jury-submission readiness or provisional release.

\*\*S11 — No broad no-flags claim with open flags present.\*\*

An audit summary may not state "No FLAGS identified" if the document contains named open flags. Required: "No additional audit failures beyond the listed open flags."

\# PART XI — META-RULE O: JURY PROTOCOL AND DISCIPLINED DEPARTURE

\*\*O1 — Standard jury prompt.\*\*

Every jury submission must include: the traversal document; the required jury report format; the classification requirement (BLOCKING DEFECT / MATERIAL REVISION / NON-BLOCKING REFINEMENT / OPEN RESEARCH RESIDUE); and the halting requirement.

\*\*O2 — Disciplined Departure.\*\*

When a traversal, audit process, or instruction-set iteration has produced N\_close consecutive certifications with no blocking defects, and when subsequent revisions primarily refine language, alignment, or presentation rather than correcting substantive failures, the system must consider Disciplined Departure.

Disciplined Departure conditions (all must hold):

1\. No current blocking defect is identified

2\. No genuinely new material defect class is identified

3\. Remaining criticisms are non-halting refinements, editorial improvements, or relabelings of previously addressed issues

4\. The process shows a pattern of diminishing marginal returns

5\. Further iteration risks increasing verbal mass, self-reference, or audit complexity more than it increases correctness

Required verdict formulation:

"Disciplined Departure is warranted. This is not a claim of absolute closure. It is a tolerance-bounded disengagement from further refinement under the present standard. The process may be reopened only by: a new blocking defect, a genuinely new material defect class, a changed target, or new external evidence."

\*\*O3 — Convergence check trigger.\*\*

After N\_substantive cycles, automatically include a convergence assessment prompt alongside any jury submission. The jury is asked to apply its own standard to its own iterative pattern.

\*\*O4 — FROZEN-PROVISIONAL status.\*\*

After Disciplined Departure, the instruction set enters FROZEN-PROVISIONAL status. New meta-rules may only be added for defect-preventing purposes — not editorial tightening or self-referential expansion.

\*\*O5 — Version increment criterion.\*\*

A new instruction set version is justified only by defect-preventing rules — rules that prevent a demonstrated blocking or material failure. Clarification, stylistic tightening, or relabeling of already-covered defects must be recorded as non-halting refinements and must not trigger a new version.

\*\*O6 — Required jury report format.\*\*

The jury verdict must use this exact structure:

1\. PUBLIC RELEASE STATUS
