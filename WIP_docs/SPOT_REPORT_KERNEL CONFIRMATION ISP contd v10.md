## **9\. Boundary image computation or obstruction**

Attempted computation:

Define a DST-derived compactification:

* Let A subset Sigma subset (S1)^4.  
* Let cl(A) be closure in (S1)^4.  
* Let Delta \= {(p,p,p,p): p in S1}.  
* Define Z0(A) \= cl(A) intersect Delta, interpreting zero diameter as total collapse.  
* Define pi\_0,j(p,p,p,p)=p.  
* Define P0 \= union\_j pi\_0,j(Z0(A)) \= {p in S1 : (p,p,p,p) in cl(A)}.

Then P0 consists exactly of points p for which there exists a sequence sigma\_n in A with all four labeled vertices tending to p.

This gives a clean conditional equivalence:

P0 subset D\_sub:  
If p in P0, then there are rectangles in S whose four vertices tend to p; their diameters tend to 0\. So p is a zero-diameter sublevel limit point.

D\_sub subset P0:  
If D\_sub is defined as points p admitting a sequence R\_n in S with diam(R\_n)-\>0 and some vertex tending to p, then all vertices also tend to p, so the corresponding sigma\_n tends to (p,p,p,p). Thus p in P0.

Therefore:  
D\_sub \= P0 is valid under the DST-derived closure in (S1)^4 if D\_sub is defined as pointwise zero-diameter vertex recurrence and if A/S correspondence preserves role-labeled parameter data.

But this does not prove P0 contains all but at most two points.

Obstruction:  
Schwartz’s Lemma 2.3 proves all-but-two coverage by V(A) using connectedness, extensiveness, and existence of arbitrarily small diameter elements. Its proof does not force the arbitrarily small elements to accumulate near each covered vertex. It uses the contrapositive: if at least three points are missing from the vertex image, every member of A must hit three disjoint arcs, forcing a uniform positive diameter lower bound.

That argument cannot be transferred to P0. Missing from P0 means “no zero-diameter sequence collapses at p,” not “p is not a vertex of any member of A.” These are different predicates.

Concrete falsification test at abstract incidence level:  
An abstract connected set A of cyclic quadrilaterals may have:

* Lambda(A) \= (0,infinity);  
* V(A) \= S1;  
* a branch of arbitrarily small-diameter quadrilaterals collapsing only at one point p0;  
* no small-diameter sequences collapsing at most other points.

Such an abstract model would satisfy “full vertex coverage plus small diameter somewhere” while P0 \= {p0}. This does not refute Schwartz’s actual geometric situation, but it shows that the source-level hypotheses used in Lemma 2.3 are insufficient to prove the Zero-Diameter Boundary Projection Theorem.
