## Abstract

This document formalizes the primitive registry synthesis reached after revising the square, triangle, and circle grid framings, and adds a gate-level registry update protocol for operational use. Its central claim is that a primitive registry system is a map-making choice that determines what kinds of relation, resonance, closure, transfer, evidence, and residue become legible. A registry does not merely relabel content. It changes the grammar of admissible evidence and therefore changes what appears locally compressible, globally stable, or difficult to derive.

The framing of consideration is imposed by an observer, instrument, organism, culture, or formal system as a registry choice. If a registry is constructed, it is still constructed from an imposed frame of consideration. This creates an infinite regress if treated as final ontology. DST therefore treats each registry as a fidelity contract within a declared frame, not as universal accounting with global fidelity.

The protocol begins from one marked referent and one degree of freedom. Mature structures such as horizontal/vertical, x/y, positive/negative, inside/outside, quadrants, and coordinate grids are downstream constructions, not primitives. The ladder builds through expression, trace, return, chirality, closure, bifurcation, perpendicularity, grid formation, polygonal expansion, overlapping circle construction, solids, and higher-dimensional nesting.

## 1. Core Correction: Do Not Start With the Completed Grid

A mature registry must not be mistaken for a primitive. The square grid, Cartesian axes, sign symmetry, quadrants, and inside/outside partition are powerful but downstream products of construction.

The primitive beginning is simpler:

- one marked referent,
- one possible expression,
- one degree of freedom.

At the root, there is no x/y, no horizontal/vertical, no positive/negative, no square, no triangle, no circle lattice, and no global inside/outside. There is only a distinguishable mark with a possible expression. Every later registry is either imposed directly or constructed from an imposed frame of consideration.

Protocol rule: do not smuggle a mature coordinate system into the primitive.

## 2. Root Primitive

The root condition is:

MARK + ONE DEGREE OF FREEDOM

A mark is a distinguishable referent. A degree of freedom is the possibility of expression from that referent. Expression produces trace. Repeated or stabilized trace produces axial relation.

Primitive notation:

R0 = marked referent
D1 = one admissible degree of freedom
E1 = expression along D1
R0 --E1--> R1

The axis is not prior to expression. It is inferred from expression.

## 3. Primitive Ladder of Construction

The ladder should be represented as constructed, not assumed:

0. Mark: a distinguishable referent appears.
1. One degree of freedom: the mark may express.
2. Trace: expression leaves a path.
3. Axis: repeated expression stabilizes as axial relation.
4. Reciprocity: the trace can be traversed outward and inward.
5. Doubling/halving: outward enumeration and inward division appear as a harmonic ladder.
6. Chirality: expression can turn.
7. Closure: turning permits return.
8. Triangle: stepwise closure produces minimal enclosure.
9. Circle: continuous turning produces smooth return.
10. Bifurcation: a second expression distinguishes itself from the first.
11. Perpendicularity: the second expression stabilizes as orthogonal contrast.
12. Square grid: equalized perpendicular axes produce coordinate registry.
13. Polygonal expansion: repeated stepwise turns produce higher polygons.
14. Overlapping circle lattice: radius transfer produces neighbor-node resonance.
15. Solid: lifted closure produces volumetric registry.
16. Higher-dimensional nesting: additional independent degrees, fibers, phases, or lifted relations extend the registry ladder.

Compact maxim:

One degree of freedom first. Axis second. Turn third. Closure fourth. Grid later.

The square does not found the ladder. The ladder constructs the square.

## 4. L-Frame and C-Frame

The primitive complement is not square versus circle. The more basic distinction is linear expression versus chiral turning.

L-frame: linear extension, axial registry, displacement, count, step, distinction.
C-frame: chiral turning, return, curvature, phase, closure, resonance.

Line gives distance. Chirality gives return.

If linear expression and chiral turning occur simultaneously, the trace becomes spiral or helical. If they occur stepwise, the trace becomes polygonal. If turning continues without stopping, the trace becomes curved or circular.

No chirality, no closure. No closure, no enclosure. No enclosure, no inside/outside.

## 5. Euclidean / Square Registry With Axis Mundi

The Euclidean or square grid should be treated as an axis-mundi registry constructed from primitive expression.

Construction path:

mark -> one degree of freedom -> axial expression -> reciprocal traversal -> bifurcation -> perpendicular stabilization -> balanced extension -> square / Cartesian registry

Only after this construction do the familiar terms become available:

- horizontal / vertical,
- x / y,
- positive / negative,
- quadrants,
- coordinate address,
- rectangular containment,
- inside / outside by coordinate partition.

These are not primitive terms. They are mature affordances of the square registry.

The square registry is best described as:

axial expression + perpendicular bifurcation + equalized bilateral measure

It excels at coordinate checking, orthogonal projection, sign symmetry, rectangular containment, and Boolean-style partition. It risks flattening curvature, phase, and spectral relation into binary or coordinate forms before those frames have been declared.

## 6. Triangle Registry and Triangular-Number Progression

The triangle registry should not be treated as a square grid with diagonals added. It encodes a different construction.

The triangle is the first stepwise polygonal closure. A line cannot enclose. Two lines do not enclose. Three segments can produce the first enclosure.

Triangle = minimal closure / triadic support / first simplex relation

The triangular grid progresses through triangular numbers. Its center is not the static Euclidean center of a square grid. Its center can appear occupied and vacant intermittently as the triangular-number construction advances. The construction re-centers or redistributes relation as the triangular field grows.

This gives the triangle registry distinct affordances:

- triadic relation,
- 60-degree adjacency,
- 120-degree recurrence,
- minimal enclosure,
- recursive subdivision,
- local mesh stability,
- intermittent center occupancy,
- simplex-style support.

The triangle stores relation as mutual support among three anchors, not primarily as bilateral opposition.

## 7. Circle Registry as Sacred-Geometry / Neighbor-Circle Lattice

The circle registry intended here is not a set of concentric rings. It is a compass construction.

Construction path:

1. Draw a circle around origin at any chosen scale. The chosen radius functions as an invariant unit for that construction.
2. Choose a point on the circumference.
3. Draw the next circle from that surface point using the same radius.
4. Let centers, intersections, and overlap lenses become new construction points.
5. Continue by same-radius neighbor-circle expansion.

Thus the circle grid is an overlapping neighbor-circle lattice, like sacred-geometry or flower-of-life construction. It grows by radius steps and local overlap, not by nested shells circumscribing one another.

Circle registry affordances:

- radius preservation,
- chiral return,
- phase relation,
- vesica / overlap lens,
- intersection nodes,
- neighbor transfer,
- inscription and circumscription,
- scale translation,
- polygon and solid projection from centers and overlap points.

This C-frame registry translates line measure into curved return without claiming that line has captured the circle. It gives a constructive lattice where resonance nodes can be mapped between linear standard and spectral continuum.

## 8. Hybrid Registry: Square Checks, Triangle Closes, Circle Returns

A useful primitive map does not marry one registry. It uses the differences between registries and records transfer cost.

Square checks: coordinate evidence, perpendicularity, bilateral measure, orthogonal verification.
Triangle closes: minimal enclosure, triadic support, simplex propagation.
Circle returns: phase, chirality, radius transfer, continuous closure, overlap-node resonance.

The hybrid registry becomes powerful where these registries agree without being collapsed into literal sameness. Agreement nodes may include:

- circle intersections that land on grid points,
- polygon vertices aligned with circle-overlap nodes,
- triangle subdivisions sharing square-coordinate addresses,
- radii that constructively inscribe or circumscribe polygonal forms,
- modular cycles that complete shared-factor or coprime return paths.

Such nodes act as registry-transfer points. They are not universal truth points. They are low-residue transfer channels between declared registries.

## 9. Registry Choice as Evidence Grammar

A stimulus field does not arrive with one universal accounting attached. An observer, sensor, culture, or formal system stores it through a registry. The registry decides what counts as evidence.

Square evidence:
coordinate agreement, orthogonal closure, signed extension, rectangular containment.

Triangle evidence:
minimal closure, triadic support, recursive subdivision, local mesh stability.

Circle evidence:
phase relation, overlap node, radius transfer, return, harmonic resonance.

Hybrid evidence:
a relation survives transfer between registries with declared residue.

Therefore:

Different registry. Different admissible evidence. Different search landscape. Different compression. Different residue.

## 10. Framing, Construction, and Infinite Regress

All framings are either imposed or constructed. If constructed, they are constructed from a prior imposed frame of consideration. This exposes an infinite regress if any one frame is treated as final ontology.

The DST correction is not to deny frames. It is to declare them.

A registry is a fidelity contract:

## 11. Registry Update Rules (Formal Gate-Level Definition)

The Primitive Registry maintains an auditable, versioned record of active primitives, registry frames, scale/grain settings, evidence grammars, transfer costs, and accumulated residue. Registry updates should therefore behave like a constructive circuit rather than a loose editorial judgment.

The gate language below is operational and semantic. It may reuse names from quantum logic, topology, and hybrid paralogic grammar, but its release status here is protocol-level unless a separate formal bridge is supplied. A gate name means: "this kind of operation must be checked, logged, and assigned residue." It does not mean the registry update has become a quantum computation or a proof of a mathematical conjecture.

### 11.1 Registry State Object

Every active registry state should contain at least these fields:

```text
REGISTRY_STATE {
  state_id
  parent_state_id
  declared_holder
  active_registry: square / triangle / circle-overlap / hybrid / other
  primitive_unit
  axis_or_trace_state
  chiral_return_state
  scale_or_grain
  evidence_grammar
  transfer_channels
  residue_register
  odome_register
  update_history
  release_status
}
```

The state object does not claim global fidelity. It records the current frame of consideration and how the system stores relation at this step.

### 11.2 Core Update Circuit

A registry update passes through the following gate sequence:

```text
INPUT
 -> Straightedge Validation Gate
 -> Compass / Superposition Intake Gate
 -> Tension Detection Gate
 -> Threshold Crossing Gate
 -> Coalescence or Cleaving Path
 -> 1/f Cascade Propagation Gate
 -> Non-Orientable Transfer Gate when needed
 -> Residue Commit and Versioning Gate
OUTPUT: updated REGISTRY_STATE + audit record
```

This sequence prevents a mature registry from silently absorbing an incoming primitive without declaring frame, scale, transfer cost, and residue.

### 11.3 Gate 1: Input Validation Gate (Straightedge Check)

Purpose:
Verify that the incoming primitive, frame imposition, or relation claim has a declared shared unit referent.

Minimum requirements:

```text
- What is the mark?
- What is the first degree of freedom?
- What trace, line, radius, circle, or unit anchors the construction?
- Which registry is receiving the update?
```

Pass condition:
The primitive can be traced to a declared unit, mark, radius, line segment, or frame anchor.

Fail condition:
The update imports a mature term such as x/y, inside/outside, positive/negative, prime, color, force, phase, or proof without declaring the registry that makes the term legible.

Failure output:

```text
HIGH-RESIDUE FLAG:
undeclared primitive unit or hidden frame imposition
```

### 11.4 Gate 2: Superposition Intake Gate (Compass / Hadamard Correspondence)

Purpose:
Register the incoming primitive before premature collapse into a single mature registry.

Operation:
Hold the primitive in provisional relation to both L-frame and C-frame encodings:

```text
L-frame possibility:
line, axis, count, step, distinction, coordinate check

C-frame possibility:
turn, return, radius, phase, overlap, resonance, closure
```

Pass condition:
The update preserves both possible readings until a gate determines which one, or which hybrid relation, carries the least residue.

Failure condition:
The update forces a chiral/phase relation into a line-only account, or forces a line/count relation into a circle-only account, before transfer cost has been evaluated.

Output:

```text
SUPERPOSED_INTAKE_RECORD {
  L_encoding
  C_encoding
  unresolved_terms
  provisional_status: HELD
}
```

### 11.5 Gate 3: Tension Detection Gate (Controlled-Perpendicular / 90-Degree Resolver)

Purpose:
Measure the cost of imposing the incoming primitive on the current registry.

Tension types:

```text
axis tension: incoming relation does not align with current axis or trace
closure tension: incoming relation requires closure not available in current frame
scale tension: incoming grain conflicts with current scale
orientation tension: handedness, phase, or direction conflicts
translation tension: relation visible in one registry fails in another
semantic tension: term carries different evidence grammar across domains
```

Compute a Lambda tension scalar:

```text
lambda_tension in [0, 1]

0 = no observed clash / clean resonance
1 = maximal clash / destructive frame imposition
```

Suggested qualitative scoring:

```text
0.00-0.20 = low residue / direct coalescence likely
0.21-0.50 = mild tension / coalescence with notes
0.51-0.75 = material tension / HELD or split recommended
0.76-1.00 = high tension / cleaving path or odome required
```

Output:

```text
TENSION_RECORD {
  lambda_tension
  tension_types
  evidence
  recommended_path
}
```

### 11.6 Gate 4: Threshold Crossing Decision

Purpose:
Determine whether the update joins the existing registry or splits into a parallel residue path.

Decision rule:

```text
if lambda_tension <= local_threshold:
    follow COALESCENCE UPDATE
else:
    follow CLEAVING PATH
```

The local threshold is registry-dependent. A square registry may tolerate coordinate tension poorly but accept orthogonal tension well. A circle-overlap registry may tolerate phase drift well but require radius/overlap continuity. A triangle registry may tolerate local branching but require closure support.

### 11.7 Coalescence Update Path

Purpose:
Merge the incoming primitive into the active registry with minimum residue.

Required actions:

```text
- update active primitive list
- update scale/grain metadata
- update evidence grammar if necessary
- add cross-reference to prior state
- record why coalescence did not overclaim
```

Output:

```text
COALESCED_STATE {
  merged_primitive
  updated_registry_state
  residual_notes
  release_status: FORMAL / MODEL / SEMANTIC / HELD-TOWARD-LOAD-BEARING
}
```

### 11.8 Cleaving Path and Odome Register

Purpose:
Preserve unresolved but meaningful tension rather than forcing premature absorption.

Trigger:

```text
lambda_tension > local_threshold
```

Actions:

```text
- create ODOME entry
- split incoming relation into parallel registry interpretations
- name what survives in each frame
- name what fails transfer
- assign bridge direction
```

ODOME definition:

```text
ODOME = persistent irreducible residue generated when a transfer between frames cannot be completed without destructive conflation.
```

Output:

```text
ODOME_ENTRY {
  source_update
  affected_registry
  unresolved_relation
  L_frame_reading
  C_frame_reading
  transfer_failure
  bridge_direction
  release_status: HELD / PARADOX-CONTAINED / AUDIT-REQUIRED
}
```

### 11.9 Gate 5: 1/f Cascade Propagation Gate

Purpose:
Propagate update effects across scale without flattening high-frequency residue into low-frequency stability.

Operational interpretation:

```text
low-frequency components = stable, load-bearing, broad-scale relation
high-frequency components = local detail, unresolved residue, finer nesting
```

Update rule:

```text
- stable components remain at current registry level
- unstable or high-resolution residue propagates to finer registry levels
- repeated broad resonance may propagate upward as a candidate load-bearing pattern
```

This gate implements scale humility. It prevents the system from treating local noise as global structure or global pattern as local proof.

Output:

```text
CASCADE_RECORD {
  stable_component
  high_frequency_residue
  propagated_levels
  updated_grain
  scale_notes
}
```

### 11.10 Gate 6: Non-Orientable Transfer Gate (Klein / Clifford Correspondence)

Purpose:
Handle cases where a relation must invert frame dominance, pass between nested levels, or preserve superposition across a registry boundary.

Use this gate when:

```text
- inside/outside reverses under transfer
- L-frame and C-frame swap dominance
- local orientation cannot be preserved globally
- the relation requires lift, twist, and reattachment
- a registry transition changes what counts as evidence
```

Output:

```text
TRANSFER_RECORD {
  source_registry
  target_registry
  orientation_change
  preserved_relation
  lost_relation
  residue
  odome_links
}
```

This gate does not erase contradiction. It records how the contradiction changes when the frame is lifted, twisted, or reattached.

### 11.11 Gate 7: Residue Commit and Versioning

Purpose:
Commit the update to an auditable state history.

Required fields:

```text
version_id
previous_version_id
timestamp_or_sequence_id
operator_or_system_actor
incoming_update
gate_path_taken
lambda_tension
threshold_used
coalescence_or_cleaving_result
odome_count
residue_summary
release_status
reopen_condition
```

No update is complete until its residue has been committed. A clean coalescence still records residue as zero or minimal.

### 11.12 Efficiency Rule: Least Cumulative Residue

When multiple update paths are possible, prefer the path with the least cumulative residue, not the path that makes the result sound most decisive.

```text
least_residue_path = path that preserves the primitive relation,
                     declares frame and scale,
                     minimizes odome generation,
                     and avoids false global closure
```

This is the operational embodiment of frame humility.

## 12. Prime / Base / P-Adic Bridge

Prime artifacts provide a mathematical foothold for the registry principle.

Primality is not created by a register. It is an irreducibility relation under factor reproduction. But prime artifacts appear differently depending on the registry through which divisibility is measured and displayed.

The p-adic frame helps explain this for a mathematically trained reader. In an ordinary metric, numbers are close when their difference is small in magnitude. In a p-adic metric, numbers are close when their difference is deeply divisible by a chosen prime p. This proves that divisibility-depth can function as a metric of nearness, depth, and scale.

A base is not only notation. A base can act as a divided measuring whole. Its primitive divisors define native resonance paths. Its residues define visible artifacts.

Counting can be read reciprocally:

- outward enumeration,
- inward division,
- doubling,
- halving,
- modular return,
- residue display.

Primality appears as the irreducible residue of the counting ladder when outward enumeration is continually tested against inward divisibility.

## 14. PvNP Application

The PvNP game should model the primitive experience of search/verification asymmetry rather than claim to solve the formal P vs NP problem.

P-like condition:
a proposed relation can be checked efficiently inside the selected registry once the certificate and frame are declared.

NP-like condition:
a relation may be globally witnessable or verifiable once found, while finding the correct registry path, closure node, or transfer point may require high search cost.

The game question becomes:

- What registry makes the relation visible?
- What registry makes the certificate checkable?
- What transfer path connects the search frame to the verification frame?
- What residue appears when the wrong registry is used?

The game should therefore model:

- primitive expression,
- registry construction,
- search through possible registry paths,
- certificate checking once a path is declared,
- transfer residue between registries.
